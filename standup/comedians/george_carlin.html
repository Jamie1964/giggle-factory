Biography

George Carlin was an American stand‑up comedian, actor, author, and social critic whose groundbreaking comedy reshaped the art form. Known for his sharp wit, fearless commentary, and legendary “Seven Dirty Words” routine, Carlin became one of the most influential and respected comedians in history. His work continues to inspire generations of performers.

Legacy

  • Released 14 HBO stand‑up specials.
  • Known for his brilliant social and political commentary.
  • His “Seven Dirty Words” routine led to a Supreme Court case.
  • Influenced countless comedians including Louis C.K., Bill Burr, and Chris Rock.
  • Considered one of the greatest stand‑ups of all time.

Fun Facts

  • Started his career as part of a comedy duo.
  • Hosted the very first episode of Saturday Night Live.
  • Published multiple bestselling books.
  • Known for his love of language and wordplay.
  • His routines remain culturally relevant decades later.
